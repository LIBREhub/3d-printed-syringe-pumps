How to build a pump

Made for infusion of microfluidics.

Based on the "Open-Source Syringe Pump" developed by Andrey Samokhin

For this section you will need:

Parts

  • 1 coupling
  • 2 guide rots
  • 2 heat inserts
  • 1 lead screw
  • 1 lead screw nut
  • 2 linear bearings
  • 2 M3 - 10mm screws
  • 6 M3 - 15mm screws
  • 6 M3 - 20mm screws
  • 10 nut

Tools

  • 1 M2.5 HEX key
  • 1 M3 HEX key
  • 1 soldering iron

Printed parts

  • 1 carriage
  • 2 knobs
  • 2 side syringe holders
  • 1 stepper union
  • 1 syringe holder

Electronic Components

  • 1 stepper motor

You should build two of them

Step 1: Assemble the coupling with the stepper motor and the lead screw

Join the mentioned elements using a M2.5 HEX key, ensuring that it is tight in all four inner screws.

Step 2: Install the guides

  • Put the guide rots into the stepper union and slide them until they reach the opposite face of it.

Press against the table to ensure that they are aligned with the face of the stepper union.

  • After placing the rots, put the two M3 nuts in their designated spaces and proceed to insert the M3 - 15mm screws and tight using the M3 HEX key.

Step 3: Screw it into the stepper

Put both of the structures together and ensuring that that the holes of the stepper union fits the ones on the stepper motor. Then put the M3 - 20mm screws into them and screw them to the end.

Step 4: Continue with the carriage

  • Place the linear bearings into the carriage and hold them placing two nuts into their designated holes and then placing two M3 - 10mm screws tightly.
  • Continue by inserting the lead screw nut into the carriage, ensuring it with two M3 - 15mm screws and two nuts in the opposite side.

Always put the screw in opposite side holes

  • This gives it stability.

  • It's also possible to put them all, but is not necessary.

Step 5: Mount the carriage

You can manually twist the lead screw to make it go through.

Step 6: Proceed with the syringe holder

Before assembling anything else this piece have to be prepared, for that is nesecary to do two steps:

Step 6,1: Heat inserts

Place the heat inserts in the two front holes of the syringe holder with the help of a soldering iron. This makes easier to screw and unscrew if needed.

Caution: The soldering iron works with high temperatures.

Step 6,1: Knobs

Be sure to clean the inner spaces of the knob before putting anything

If not it will break.

Prepare the two knobs placing a nut in each knob space and then screwing the M3 - 20mm screws and tight with the HEX key.

Step 7: Place the side holders

Put one of the two side syringe holders in place and screw the knobs into the heat inserts.

Step 8: Place it into the guide rots

Press against the table to ensure that they are aligned with the face of the Syringe holder.

Slide the piece and screw it like the previous ones using two M3 - 15mm screws with the nuts.

Now you have a full pump assembled and ready to be used.
